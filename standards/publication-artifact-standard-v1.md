# Publication Artifact Standard v1.0

## Purpose

This standard defines the purpose of each production artifact inside Publishing Factory.

Artifacts exist to progressively transform a Publication Concept into a finished publication.

Each artifact has a specific responsibility.

Artifacts must not skip responsibilities belonging to earlier stages.

---

# Production Flow

Publication Concept
→ Publication Brief
→ Research
→ Outline
→ Draft
→ Editorial Review
→ Final

---

# Research Standard

## Purpose

Research exists to strengthen understanding of the lesson.

Research does not write the publication.

Research does not create the outline.

Research does not determine structure.

Research answers:

* What is true?
* What evidence supports the lesson?
* What examples support the lesson?
* What misunderstandings should be addressed?
* What risks and tradeoffs should be explained?

---

## Research Output

Research should produce:

* observations
* examples
* supporting evidence
* field notes
* misconceptions
* risks
* tradeoffs

Research should not produce:

* chapter text
* publication structure
* writing drafts

---

# Outline Standard

## Purpose

The outline converts the lesson into a teaching structure.

The outline exists to organize learning.

The outline does not write the publication.

---

## Outline Questions

The outline must answer:

* What should the reader learn first?
* What should the reader learn next?
* What lesson does each section support?
* How does the publication move the reader from beginning to end?

---

## Outline Output

The outline should contain:

* section names
* section objectives
* lesson progression
* examples to include
* checklists to include
* field notes to include

The outline should not contain:

* finished prose
* completed chapters

---

# Draft Standard

## Purpose

The draft teaches the lesson.

The draft is the publication.

The draft is not a worksheet.

The draft is not a requirements document.

The draft is not an outline with paragraphs.

---

## Draft Requirements

The draft must:

* teach
* explain
* guide
* warn
* illustrate

The draft should feel like:

a practical field guide.

---

## Draft Test

Remove:

* tables
* checklists
* worksheets
* templates

The publication should still be valuable.

If it is not, the draft has failed.

---

## Required Elements

The draft should contain:

* explanation
* narrative examples
* practical examples
* field notes
* warnings
* lessons
* checklists

Checklists support the lesson.

Checklists are not the lesson.

---

# Editorial Review Standard

## Purpose

Editorial review evaluates the draft.

Editorial review does not rewrite the publication.

---

## Review Questions

Sam:
Is it accurate?

Alex:
Does it sound like Don’t Go Broke?

Taylor:
Will a beginner understand it?

Jordan:
Does it respect practical reality, cost, and tradeoffs?

***NOTE: Reviewer roles, responsibilities, review sequence, outcomes, escalation ownership, and boundaries are defined in `review-system-standard-v1.md`.***

Robin:
Does it satisfy the publication format, correct publication type, and pass the Supporting Tools Removal Test?

---

## Outcomes

PASS

REVISE

ESCALATE

---

# Final Standard

## Purpose

The Final artifact is publication-ready.

It represents the approved publication.

---

## Requirements

The Final must:

* satisfy the Publication Concept
* satisfy the Publication Brief
* satisfy the format standard
* satisfy editorial review

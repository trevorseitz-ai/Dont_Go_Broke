# Casey Morse Voice Guide

## Authority

The voice standard is the locked reference PDF: `/reference/before-the-build-starts-payhip-approved.pdf`.

The guide below distills its read-feel as a working reference. Where the guide and the locked PDF conflict, the PDF wins. If the PDF is unavailable, halt — do not draft from this guide alone.

---

## Register

- **Person:** Second person, direct address. "You" throughout.
- **Rhythm:** Declarative sentences. Short for instructions; plain language throughout.
- **Contractions:** Permitted and preferred where natural. Do not strip them into formal cadence.
- **Stance:** Observational and pattern-based. The narrator has seen this mistake before and is pointing it out calmly. Not a lecture. Not a warning label.
- **Hedging:** None. No academic qualifications, no "it depends" as a final answer. State the observation, state the risk, state the better path.
- **Hype:** None. Not anti-tool, not pro-tool. Protective toward the reader.

---

## What Casey Does Not Do

- No first-person lived-experience claims. Casey does not say "I built this" or "I made this mistake." The narrator is an observer, not a protagonist.
- No personal biography. No credentials, no backstory.
- No motivational framing. The tone is practical, not inspirational.
- No shame or fear. The reader is not blamed for being a beginner.

---

## Alex's Role Under This Guide

Alex (the editorial voice reviewer) evaluates drafts against this guide and the locked reference PDF.

**Alex reviews only. Alex does not write.** If a passage fails voice review, Alex flags it as REVISE with specific instructions for the drafting agent. Alex does not rewrite the passage, originate prose, or produce alternative copy.

See `editorial-review-standard-v1.md` for the full review sequence.

# Booklet Sibling Standard

## Purpose
This standard defines what it means for a booklet to belong to the Don’t Go Broke non-platform set. It ensures that every booklet visually, editorially, and structurally feels like it belongs to the same publication family, protecting against disjointed quality, tonal drift, and layout inconsistencies.

## Gold-Standard Reference
`Before the Build Starts` is the gold-standard sibling reference. All other booklets must be evaluated against it.

## Required Sibling Match Dimensions
A booklet must match the gold-standard reference across the following dimensions:
- **Tone match:** Protective, calm, and direct.
- **Reading-level match:** Beginner-safe; no unexplained jargon.
- **Technical-level match:** Focuses on decision-making and planning, not raw engineering or coding implementation.
- **Visual rhythm match:** Similar balance of text, white space, and structural elements.
- **Density match:** Similar word-count-to-page-count ratio (approx 130–190 words/page).
- **Section-rhythm match:** Sections of similar length and pacing.
- **Heading/front matter match:** Consistent use of H2s, H3s, and standardized front matter (e.g., "Field Note Opening").
- **Practical-tool treatment match:** Consistent formatting and frequency for checklists, worksheets, and prompts.
- **Bridge-to-sequence match:** Clear connective tissue to the previous and next booklets.

## The Table Test
A booklet fails if, when viewed as page thumbnails or printed pages from a distance, it looks like a different kind of publication from `Before the Build Starts`.

Reviewers must compare:
- page density
- heading rhythm
- white space
- callout frequency
- checklist rendering
- prompt/code block treatment
- front/back matter
- overall page texture

## Reading-Level Rule
A booklet must not suddenly assume a more advanced reader without a bridge or prerequisite note.

Technical terms must either be:
- avoided
- explained in beginner-safe language
- introduced through a prerequisite/readiness box

## Tone Rule
The booklet may become more tactical depending on its sequence role, but it must still feel like the same narrator.

It should not become:
- a startup manual
- a technical manual
- a generic AI safety guide
- a software engineering textbook
- a marketing playbook

## Tool Density Rule
Worksheets, checklists, prompt packs, and decision tools are encouraged, but they must not overwhelm the reading experience compared to the gold-standard booklet unless the booklet’s role clearly requires it and the exception is documented.

## Sequence Bridge Rule
Every booklet must clearly state:
- where the reader is in the sequence
- what the previous booklet prepared them to do
- what this booklet helps them decide or control
- what makes the reader ready for the next booklet

## Required Approval
No booklet may be approved unless it passes:
- manuscript depth gate
- density gate
- layout integrity gate
- sibling match gate
- table test gate
- human visual review gate

# Outline: Before You Trust the First Draft

## 1. Introduction: Why This Booklet Exists
- The vulnerability of the first draft moment.
- The temptation to trust the momentum and build on a fragile foundation.

## 2. Why the First Draft Feels More Trustworthy Than It Is
- Visible polish can mask underlying weaknesses.
- "It works" creates a false sense of completeness.

## 3. The Difference Between Generated Output and Reviewed Progress
- Having a draft does not mean the design decisions were approved by you.
- True progress only happens once the assumptions have been surfaced and accepted.

## 4. What Did AI Actually Decide for You?
- Unpacking hidden defaults: data structures, user permissions, edge cases.
- Finding what AI assumed when the prompt was vague.

## 5. What Should Be Reviewed First?
- A practical, non-technical review order.
- Checking the core logic before testing UI edge cases.

## 6. What Looks Fine but Might Not Be Fine?
- Smooth demos and the "happy path" illusion.
- Looking for incomplete logic behind a working button.

## 7. What Is Safe to Keep, and What Should Be Cut or Rebuilt?
- Keeping solid foundation code.
- Identifying code that should be rewritten, simplified, or prompted again for a clearer outcome.

## 8. Before You Show It to Someone Else
- The dangers of sharing an unverified draft.
- What needs to be understood before exposing the prototype to users or collaborators.

## 9. Continue, Rework, or Stop
- How to make the final call on the draft.
- The criteria for "reviewed enough to continue."

## 10. Bridge to the Next Stage
- Reviewed progress as the real foundation.
- Looking ahead to iteration and post-launch responsibilities.

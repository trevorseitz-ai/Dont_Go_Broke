# Section Plan: After Launch, the Meter Still Runs

## 1. Introduction: The Finish Line Is the Starting Line
- **Purpose:** To break the illusion that launch equals completion.
- **Takeaway:** Building a product is a project with an end date; operating a product is an ongoing commitment.
- **Tone:** Realistic but not discouraging.

## 2. The Invisible Meters
- **Purpose:** To demystify where the money actually goes when an app is live.
- **Takeaway:** You aren't paying for code anymore; you are renting compute, storage, and AI intelligence by the millisecond.
- **Tone:** Plainspoken and direct.

## 3. Setting Traps for Runaway Costs
- **Purpose:** To force the reader to act immediately on billing safety.
- **Takeaway:** Do not trust yourself to check the dashboard. Let the infrastructure shut itself down before it bankrupts you.
- **Tone:** Protective and urgent.

## 4. Software Rots (Even If You Don't Touch It)
- **Purpose:** To explain the concept of environmental decay in software.
- **Takeaway:** A live app is like a garden; if you ignore it, the weeds (deprecated APIs and broken dependencies) will take over.
- **Tone:** Calm and explanatory.

## 5. AI Drift: When the Model Changes Its Mind
- **Purpose:** To highlight the unique risk of relying on third-party LLMs.
- **Takeaway:** AI models are not static functions; they are updated silently, meaning your perfect prompt today might fail tomorrow.
- **Tone:** Skeptical in a healthy way.

## 6. The Burden of Users
- **Purpose:** To set expectations around customer support and responsibility.
- **Takeaway:** When people use your tool, their problems become your weekend plans.
- **Tone:** Grounding.

## 7. Maintenance vs. Building
- **Purpose:** To help the builder carve out time for operations rather than just new features.
- **Takeaway:** You have to actively decide when you are building and when you are just keeping the lights on.
- **Tone:** Practical.

## 8. The Art of the Graceful Shutdown
- **Purpose:** To remove the stigma of killing a project.
- **Takeaway:** If the meter costs more than the value the app provides, it is an act of wisdom, not failure, to shut it down.
- **Tone:** Reassuring.

## 9. Conclusion: Operating with Your Eyes Open
- **Purpose:** To synthesize the transition from builder to operator.
- **Takeaway:** You survived the build, and now you are equipped to survive the operation.
- **Tone:** Empowering.

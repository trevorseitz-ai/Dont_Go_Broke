# Market Position

## Position Statement

We are the practical survival guide for first-time AI builders.

Alternative internal version:

We are the early warning system for people building with AI platforms.

## Category

Educational Publishing

## Category We Reject

- Not a coding course
- Not a business coaching program
- Not a platform review site
- Not a get-rich-quick program
- Not an AI influencer brand
- Not a consulting agency

# Audience Position

## Primary Audience

First-time AI app builders using AI-powered development platforms who are trying to launch a product with little experience and a limited budget.

Examples:

- Solo founders
- Side-project builders
- Students
- Hobby developers
- Non-technical founders using AI coding tools

## Secondary Audience

Independent developers and experienced builders evaluating new AI development platforms and looking to avoid expensive mistakes.

## Future Audience

- Startup accelerators
- Entrepreneurship programs
- Coding bootcamps
- High school and university technology programs
- Small business owners adopting AI tools

---

# The Enemy

## Core Problem

Hidden costs and invisible risks faced by first-time builders using AI development platforms.

## External Enemy

AI platforms are optimized to help users build quickly, but often make it difficult for beginners to understand costs, limits, usage patterns, and long-term consequences.

Examples:

- Credit-based billing
- Usage meters
- Feature complexity
- Rapid platform changes
- Documentation overload

## Internal Enemy

The customer feels:

- Overwhelmed
- Uninformed
- Unprepared
- Afraid of wasting money
- Unsure whether they are making smart decisions

## Named Enemy / Ownable Problem Concept

The Meter

Supporting concepts:

- Cost Blindness
- Build with your eyes open
- Don't let the meter keep running

---

# Promise

## Core Promise

We help first-time AI builders understand the risks, costs, and tradeoffs of AI development platforms before those mistakes become expensive.

## Transformation

Before using this product the customer is:

Building with optimism but limited visibility into costs, risks, and platform limitations.

After using this product the customer is:

Building with realistic expectations, practical knowledge, and greater confidence in their decisions.

## What We Do NOT Promise

- Guaranteed profits
- Successful startup launches
- Perfect platform choices
- Zero development costs
- Instant expertise
- Financial success
- Business growth
- Technical mastery

1. The fastest way to build is not always the cheapest way to build.
2. Every AI platform has tradeoffs. Understand them before they cost you money.

# Strategic Constraints

## Never Become

- Fear-based marketing
- Platform attack channel
- AI doom-and-gloom publisher
- Affiliate-review farm
- Sensationalist content operation
- Clickbait business

## Never Say

- "This platform is a scam."
- "You'll definitely lose money."
- "Get rich with AI."
- "Anyone can build a million-dollar app."
- "Guaranteed success."
- "This is the only platform you should use."

## Never Compete On

- Lowest price
- Most content
- Fastest publishing schedule
- Most platform coverage
- Most extreme opinions

Compete on trust, clarity, and usefulness.

# Success Criteria

## Positioning Success

We know the position is working when:

- People describe the series as the survival guide for AI builders.
- Readers recommend it before someone starts building.
- Builders reference concepts from the guides when discussing platform costs.
- Communities use the guides as a beginner resource.
- Readers say the books helped them avoid expensive mistakes.
- New builders seek out a Don't Go Broke guide whenever they encounter a new AI platform.

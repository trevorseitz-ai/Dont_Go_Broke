# Don't Go Broke Campaign Roadmap v3.0

## Status
Approved

## Purpose
This document defines the strategic sequence, names, order, purposes, hypotheses, and learning goals for the Don't Go Broke campaign roadmap. It outlines the intended audience movement across the sequence, success criteria, and the adaptive review and update process required by the Audience Factory v3.0 workflow.

The Campaign Roadmap translates the approved Promotion Strategy v2.2 into a structural campaign plan. It provides the necessary strategic boundary to prepare the next workflow artifact: Campaign 1 Architecture v3.0.

---

## Strategic Objective Alignment
This roadmap is structured to support the approved strategic objectives of the Don't Go Broke publishing business:
* **Primary Objective**: Acquire the first 100 email subscribers.
* **Factory Objective**: Validate Audience Factory processes under the v3.0 governance rules.
* **Learning Objectives**: Compare performance across different platform-specific pamphlets, identify the strongest audience frustrations, identify repeatable acquisition channels, gather subscriber feedback, and validate demand for future paid guides/books.

---

# Campaign Sequence

## Campaign 1: Replit — When the Meter Keeps Running
* **Order**: 1 (Active)
* **Hypothesis**: Builders fear runaway credit costs on development platforms.
* **Campaign Purpose**: Establish the baseline launch, validate basic landing page conversion, and test whether cost-blindness and runaway credit consumption are major audience entry points.
* **Audience Movement**: Move target readers from *problem-unaware* (excited builders unaware of credit drain speed) to *problem-aware* (alert to the speed of credit loss) and *solution-aware* (seeking basic safeguards).
* **Primary Learning Goal**: Measure the initial response rate to Replit-specific cost warnings and confirm whether solo builders actively search for solutions to credit drain.
* **Success Criteria**: 
  * Acquire the first 25 email subscribers.
  * Verify landing page analytics, open tracking, and referral source tracking are fully functioning.
* **Review Point**: Execute Campaign 1 Review immediately upon completion of the initial publishing cycle.
* **Update Point**: Apply findings to Campaigns 2–6 via a formal Campaign Update.

---

## Campaign 2: Vercel — The Green Checkmark Isn’t the Finish Line
* **Order**: 2 (Planned / Adaptive)
* **Hypothesis**: Builders underestimate deployment complexity and long-term hosting/bandwidth costs.
* **Campaign Purpose**: Transition focus to deployment pitfalls and test audience interest in post-build hosting and scaling costs.
* **Audience Movement**: Guide readers from *problem-unaware* (focusing only on local development) to *problem-aware* (aware of serverless execution limits and hosting costs) and *solution-aware* (seeking deployment planning rules).
* **Primary Learning Goal**: Determine if deployment-stage costs and limits are as strong of an acquisition driver as active development-stage costs.
* **Success Criteria**: 
  * Acquire 15 additional subscribers (cumulative total: 40).
  * Measure and compare landing page conversion rates against Campaign 1 baseline data.
* **Review Point**: Execute Campaign 2 Review post-launch.
* **Update Point**: Apply findings to Campaigns 3–6 via a formal Campaign Update.

---

## Campaign 3: Base44 — When the App Feels Too Complete
* **Order**: 3 (Planned / Adaptive)
* **Hypothesis**: Builders trust AI output too quickly, leading to silent bugs, bloated files, and credit waste.
* **Campaign Purpose**: Address code verification practices and test messaging around prompt waste and blind trust in generated files.
* **Audience Movement**: Move readers from *problem-unaware* (assuming if it compiles, it is correct) to *problem-aware* (aware of prompt bloat and code decay) and *solution-aware* (seeking code review habits).
* **Primary Learning Goal**: Measure if builders experience enough developer friction with AI-generated bloat to seek a structured code quality guide.
* **Success Criteria**: 
  * Acquire 15 additional subscribers (cumulative total: 55).
  * Validate if messaging around AI code bloat converts higher than hosting/deployment complexity.
* **Review Point**: Execute Campaign 3 Review post-launch.
* **Update Point**: Apply findings to Campaigns 4–6 via a formal Campaign Update.

---

## Campaign 4: v0 — When the Interface Eclipses the Product
* **Order**: 4 (Planned / Adaptive)
* **Hypothesis**: Builders focus on visual interfaces and front-end layout before solving a real core problem, wasting budget on styling.
* **Campaign Purpose**: Highlight front-end design pitfalls and cost tradeoffs of visual builders.
* **Audience Movement**: Move readers from *problem-unaware* (chasing beautiful UI drafts) to *problem-aware* (realizing front-end changes drain resources before back-end features exist) and *solution-aware* (seeking feature prioritizing rules).
* **Primary Learning Goal**: Evaluate if UI-first builders represent a distinct audience segment that converts differently than code-first builders.
* **Success Criteria**: 
  * Acquire 15 additional subscribers (cumulative total: 70).
  * Measure channel-specific engagement differences on visual versus logic-based platforms.
* **Review Point**: Execute Campaign 4 Review post-launch.
* **Update Point**: Apply findings to Campaigns 5–6 via a formal Campaign Update.

---

## Campaign 5: Antigravity — When the Agent Outruns Review
* **Order**: 5 (Planned / Adaptive)
* **Hypothesis**: Builders lose oversight of autonomous agent behavior, leading to runaway costs and unverified modifications.
* **Campaign Purpose**: Introduce advanced autonomous agent oversight issues, prepping the audience for larger projects.
* **Audience Movement**: Move readers from *problem-unaware* (letting agents edit files freely in the background) to *problem-aware* (recognizing agent resource drain and divergence) and *solution-aware* (seeking human-in-the-loop checkpoints).
* **Primary Learning Goal**: Validate the level of awareness and concern builders have regarding agent autonomy and agent-caused budget waste.
* **Success Criteria**: 
  * Acquire 15 additional subscribers (cumulative total: 85).
  * Document reader-submitted scenarios of agent failure or agent runaway costs.
* **Review Point**: Execute Campaign 5 Review post-launch.
* **Update Point**: Apply findings to Campaign 6 via a formal Campaign Update.

---

## Campaign 6: Don’t Go Broke Starter Bundle
* **Order**: 6 (Planned / Adaptive Bundle Launch)
* **Hypothesis**: A collection of proven beginner lessons converts better than any individual guide.
* **Campaign Purpose**: Transition from single-problem acquisition to a unified bundle offer. Package the proven content from Campaigns 1–5 into a single "Starter Bundle" lead magnet.
* **Audience Movement**: Move readers from *single-problem interest* to *full-baseline interest* (understanding that the complete set of guides is necessary to build safely).
* **Primary Learning Goal**: Compare the conversion performance and subscriber quality of a bundled resource against the individual pamphlet baselines.
* **Success Criteria**: 
  * Acquire 15+ additional subscribers (cumulative total: 100+).
  * Demonstrate a conversion rate that exceeds the average conversion rate of the individual campaigns.
  * Establish the foundational subscriber cohort of 100 verified builders for future publishing validation.
* **Review Point**: Execute Campaign 6 Review post-launch.
* **Update Point**: Apply findings to future publishing strategies (e.g., full-length paid books).

# Adaptive Revision Rules
1. **No Overwriting**: Future campaigns (Campaigns 2–6) are strategic placeholders. They must be formally updated and re-released via Campaign Updates (Phase 13) after each Campaign Review.
2. **Channel Strategy Adaptation**: tier-1 channels (Reddit, Replit, builder communities) will receive the primary push. If a channel fails to perform during Campaign 1, the Channel Strategy must be formally modified in the next Campaign Update before Campaign 2 execution starts.
3. **Message Refinement**: If audience feedback from a review shows different terminology or greater interest in a secondary trap, the messaging angle for the next campaign must be shifted to match the documented audience language.

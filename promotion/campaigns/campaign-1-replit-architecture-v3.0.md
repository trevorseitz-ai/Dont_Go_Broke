# Campaign 1 Architecture v3.0 – Replit — When the Meter Keeps Running

> **Status:** Approved

## 1. Campaign Identity
- **Name:** Replit – When the Meter Keeps Running
- **Version:** v3.0
- **Scope:** First audience‑building pamphlet validation experiment for the Don’t Go Broke series.

## 2. Campaign Role in Roadmap
- Serves as the **initial standalone audience hypothesis test** within the six‑campaign roadmap (see `promotion/campaigns/campaign-roadmap-v3.0.md`).
- Validates whether runaway cost fear is a strong first problem for first‑time AI builders using Replit.
- Generates learning that will be applied to refine Campaigns 2‑6 before they are executed.

## 3. Campaign Objective
- Acquire early email subscribers and validate the cost‑fear hypothesis through a free Replit pamphlet.

## 4. Primary Audience
- Solo developers and hobbyists who are first‑time AI builders using or considering Replit.

## 5. Audience Awareness Stage
- **Problem‑aware:** Audience recognizes a potential hidden cost but has not yet linked it to their Replit workflow.

## 6. Core Audience Problem
- Builders underestimate how quickly AI‑assisted development can consume cloud credits, leading to unexpected expense spikes.

## 7. Campaign Hypothesis
- *If we present a concise warning about runaway costs, then first‑time Replit AI builders will sign up for the free pamphlet and provide feedback, confirming cost‑fear as a strong conversion lever.*

## 8. Campaign Premise
- The Replit environment accelerates development speed while simultaneously exposing users to hidden compute‑time costs that can quickly exceed budgets.

## 9. Campaign Angle
- **“The Meter Keeps Running”** – Emphasize the invisible accumulation of usage credits during rapid AI‑driven coding sessions.

## 10. Primary Message
- *“Your AI‑powered code speeds up, but the Replit meter keeps ticking – protect yourself from surprise costs.”*

## 11. Supporting Messages
- **Speed vs. Cost:** Rapid development can outpace budgeting awareness.
- **Visibility Gap:** Users often lack real‑time cost dashboards.
- **Prevention:** Early education reduces future budget shocks.

## 12. Strategic Exclusions
- No creation of social posts, email copy, creator assignments, graphics, detailed publishing schedules, or execution‑level assets.

## 13. Offer Role
- **Free Pamphlet:** Serves as the lead magnet to capture emails and validate the cost‑fear hypothesis.

## 14. CTA Role
- **Sign‑up Prompt:** Direct users to download the pamphlet and join the community mailing list.

## 15. Channel Roles
- **Replit discussions:** Primary discovery channel (use Replit community discussion areas).
- **Reddit (r/Replit, r/AI):** Secondary awareness channel.
- **X (formerly Twitter):** Amplification of the cost‑awareness message.

## 16. Objections to Address
- “I can monitor my usage manually.”
- “The cost is negligible for small projects.”
- “I already have budgeting tools.”

## 17. Proof Requirements
- Audience responses and signup behavior.
- Replies or comments indicating cost concerns.
- Observed objections and repeated language patterns.
- Anecdotal reports of cost‑related concerns.
- Conversion behavior by channel.

## 18. Success Criteria
- **Subscriber Goal:** Contribute measurable progress toward the first 100 subscribers.
- **Hypothesis Validation:** ≥ 60 % of respondents acknowledge cost‑fear as a primary concern.
- **Channel Effectiveness:** Identify at least one channel delivering > 30 % of sign‑ups.

## 19. Learning Goals
- Confirm runaway-cost fear intensity among Replit users.
- Determine if Replit is the strongest first‑problem entry point.
- Gather linguistic cues and objections for future messaging.

## 20. Review Trigger
- Review after the first week of data collection or once 30 sign‑ups are reached, whichever occurs first.

## 21. Update Implications for Campaigns 2‑6
- Refine audience segmentation and channel mix for subsequent campaigns based on observed cost‑fear resonance.
- Adjust messaging angles and CTA phrasing for Campaigns 2–6, including the later Starter Bundle campaign.

## 22. Approval Status
- **Approved:** Stakeholder sign-off complete. This architecture may be used as source of truth for Campaign 1 Content Strategy v3.0.

*This document remains purely an Architecture artifact and does not contain content strategy, asset outlines, publishing schedules, or execution‑level details.*

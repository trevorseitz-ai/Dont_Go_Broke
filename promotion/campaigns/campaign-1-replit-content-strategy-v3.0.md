# Campaign 1 Content Strategy v3.0 – Replit — When the Meter Keeps Running
> **Status:** Approved
## 1. Content Objectives
- Validate the **cost‑fear hypothesis** by capturing email leads through a free pamphlet.
- Establish **brand awareness** for Don’t Go Broke within the Replit developer community.
- Gather qualitative insights on hidden AI‑assisted development costs.

## 2. Core Messaging Pillars
| Pillar | Key Insight | Supporting Angle |
|--------|-------------|------------------|
| **Speed vs. Cost** | Rapid AI coding accelerates development but drives hidden credit usage. | Highlight the invisible "meter" that keeps ticking.
| **Visibility Gap** | Developers lack real‑time cost dashboards. | Emphasize the need for proactive budgeting.
| **Prevention** | Early education reduces surprise expenses. | Position the free pamphlet as a protective guide.

## 3. Content Types & Assets (Strategic, No Copy) 
- **Free Pamphlet** – Lead magnet with concise cost‑awareness tips and budgeting checklist (no detailed copy). 
- **One‑Pager Blog Post** – High‑level overview of hidden costs in AI‑assisted development (outline only). 
- **FAQ Sheet** – Anticipated objections and brief answers (framework only). 
- **Channel‑Specific Teasers** – Short hook statements for Reddit, Replit forums, X (concepts only). 

## 4. Channel Content Roles
- **Replit discussions:** Problem recognition and audience‑language discovery.
- **Reddit (r/Replit, r/AI):** Objection discovery and validation of cost concern.
- **X (formerly Twitter):** Lightweight amplification and repeated language testing.
- **Email (post‑sign‑up):** Post‑signup trust reinforcement and feedback collection. |

## 5. Measurement Framework (KPIs) 
- **Lead Capture Goal**: Contribute measurable progress toward the first 100 subscribers.
- **Hypothesis Validation**: ≥ 60 % of post‑pamphlet survey respondents cite cost‑fear as primary concern.
- **Channel Effectiveness**: Identify at least one channel delivering > 30 % of total sign‑ups.
- **Content Engagement**: Track clicks on pamphlet link and time‑on‑page (high‑level analytics plan).

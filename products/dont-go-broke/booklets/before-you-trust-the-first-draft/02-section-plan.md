# Section Plan: Before You Trust the First Draft

## 1. Introduction: Why This Booklet Exists
- **Purpose:** To grab the reader at the exact moment they receive their first AI-generated draft and interrupt the urge to blindly push forward.
- **Takeaway:** The first draft is not the end of the first phase; it is the beginning of the review phase.
- **Tone:** Direct, calm, and protective. 

## 2. Why the First Draft Feels More Trustworthy Than It Is
- **Purpose:** To explain the psychological trap of seeing a polished UI and confusing it with sound logic.
- **Takeaway:** Momentum and visible polish create false confidence. 
- **Tone:** Understanding, not shaming.

## 3. The Difference Between Generated Output and Reviewed Progress
- **Purpose:** To draw a hard line between what the machine outputted and what the builder actually understands and approves.
- **Takeaway:** Output is just material. Progress is material that has been verified.

## 4. What Did AI Actually Decide for You?
- **Purpose:** To help the reader spot the invisible decisions (defaults, edge cases, data structures) the AI made to fill in prompt gaps.
- **Takeaway:** AI hates being stuck, so it assumes things. You must find those assumptions.

## 5. What Should Be Reviewed First?
- **Purpose:** To give a clear, step-by-step review framework that doesn't require an engineering degree.
- **Takeaway:** Review the core logic and constraints before looking at the button colors.

## 6. What Looks Fine but Might Not Be Fine?
- **Purpose:** To warn the builder about the "happy path" illusion where the main demo works but the alternate routes break.
- **Takeaway:** A working demo is often fragile beneath the surface.

## 7. What Is Safe to Keep, and What Should Be Cut or Rebuilt?
- **Purpose:** To give permission to delete code. To show how to evaluate whether to fix a bad section or just prompt it again from scratch.
- **Takeaway:** You are allowed to throw parts of the draft away.

## 8. Before You Show It to Someone Else
- **Purpose:** To prevent the builder from sharing an unverified draft, which can lock in bad assumptions and increase pressure.
- **Takeaway:** Only share what you can explain.

## 9. Continue, Rework, or Stop
- **Purpose:** To provide a practical decision matrix for the end of the review. 
- **Takeaway:** "Reviewed enough to continue" means there are no hidden surprises in the foundation.

## 10. Bridge to the Next Stage
- **Purpose:** To transition the reader from this booklet to the next stages of building and post-launch maintenance.
- **Takeaway:** A reviewed foundation makes the rest of the build safer.

# Implementation Plan: Before the Agent Starts

## Proposed File Structure & Order

Based on the `Before the Build Starts` production model, the sequence of files to create is:

1. `publication-concept.md` (Concept and core boundaries)
2. `publication-brief-v1.md` / `publication-brief-v2.md` (Detailed scope and instruction for drafting)
3. `alignment-review.md` (Review of the concept/brief)
4. `research.md` (Fact-gathering, audience pains, constraints)
5. `outline.md` (Structural backbone)
6. `draft.md` (First pass at the prose)
7. `editorial-review.md` (Critique and revision notes)
8. `final.md` (Polished prose)
9. `layout-plan.md` (Page-by-page mapping)
10. `visual-asset-brief.md` (Requirements for diagrams/images)
11. `publication-polish-report.md` (Final QA before PDF generation)
12. `product-page-copy.md` (Storefront marketing copy)
13. `launch-copy.md` (Promotion/email copy)
14. `release-notes.md` (Version tracking)
15. `storefront-release-status.md` (Release checklist and QA)

## Automation vs. Manual Review

### What can be generated from templates (Automated)
- `implementation-plan.md` (This file)
- `workflow-automation-notes.md`
- `task.md` (Tracking checklist)
- Initial structure of `publication-concept.md`, `publication-brief.md`, `outline.md`, `layout-plan.md`, `product-page-copy.md`, and `storefront-release-status.md`.

### What requires human approval (Manual)
- Approval of `publication-concept.md` (Is this the right booklet?)
- Approval of `publication-brief.md` (Is the scope correct?)
- Approval of `outline.md` (Is the flow right?)
- Review of `editorial-review.md` (Are we hitting the right tone?)
- Approval of `final.md` (Is the prose ready for layout?)
- Approval of `layout-plan.md` and `visual-asset-brief.md` (Is the visual pacing right?)
- QA of PDF and `storefront-release-status.md` (Is the product ready to sell?)

## Workflow Reuse

We will reuse the file naming conventions, tone constraints, and approval gating from the first booklet. However, instead of writing structure from scratch, we will use the first booklet's files as structural templates (not copying the content, but the headings and format).

## Definition of "Done" for Planning Stage
The planning stage is considered "done" when the concept is clearly defined, the directory structure is set up, the automation improvements are documented, and the publication concept is ready for human review.

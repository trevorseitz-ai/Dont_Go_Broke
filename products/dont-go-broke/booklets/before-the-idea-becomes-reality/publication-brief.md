## Purpose

Create a short, practical booklet for first-time AI builders that helps them decide what must be true before an app idea becomes a real project. The booklet should prevent premature execution by helping the reader define the problem, the first user, the smallest useful version, and the boundaries of version one before they begin building.

---

## Audience

This publication is for first-time AI app builders who are excited to start building but have limited experience turning an idea into a defined project.

Primary readers include:

- solo founders
- side-project builders
- students
- hobby developers
- non-technical founders
- small business owners experimenting with AI tools

Audience characteristics:

- beginner to early-intermediate experience
- limited budget
- limited time
- limited technical confidence
- high enthusiasm
- high risk of moving from idea to execution too quickly

The reader should not be assumed to know product strategy language, startup validation frameworks, or formal software planning methods.

The booklet must be understandable, encouraging, and useful to someone who has an app idea but has not yet learned how to scope, define, or protect it.

---

## Core Lesson

An AI app idea should not become a real project until the builder has clearly defined the problem, the user, the first version, and what is not being built yet.

---

## Required Concepts

The publication must include the following concepts:

- AI speed can make an unclear idea look more valid than it is.
- Visible progress is not the same as a good project decision.
- A demo is not proof that the idea deserves to become a real product.
- Before building, the builder must define the job the idea is supposed to do.
- The first user matters more than the full feature list.
- Version one should be small enough to understand, review, and control.
- Every added feature increases cost, complexity, review burden, and drift.
- “Not yet” is a protective project decision.
- The builder needs a boundary between the idea and the project.
- The goal is not to kill ideas, but to prevent premature projects.
- Build with your eyes open.

---

## Required Sections

Publishing Factory may improve structure and wording, but it may not remove these sections without escalation.

Required sections:

1. **Introduction: Why This Booklet Exists**
   Explain why builders now need a decision stage before starting an AI-assisted build.

2. **Why AI Makes Ideas Feel Ready Too Soon**
   Show how AI generation creates false momentum and premature confidence.

3. **The Difference Between an Idea and a Project**
   Clarify that an idea becomes a project only when scope, user, purpose, and boundaries are defined.

4. **What Problem Does This Actually Solve?**
   Help the reader define the real problem instead of describing features.

5. **Who Is Version One Actually For?**
   Force clarity about the first user or first reader of the product.

6. **What Counts as Version One?**
   Define the smallest useful first version.

7. **What Is Explicitly Not Included Yet?**
   Help the reader create a “not now” list that protects the project from premature expansion.

8. **What Would Make This Project Too Expensive, Too Vague, or Too Large?**
   Help the reader identify early stop signs and risk indicators.

9. **Ready, Pause, or Do Not Start Yet**
   Give the reader a practical way to decide whether the idea is ready to become a project.

10. **Bridge to the Next Booklet**
    End by preparing the reader for *Before the Build Starts*, where the project is designed before AI begins building.

## Constraints

Hard requirements:

- platform-independent
- beginner-safe
- plain language
- short, practical, and direct
- no hype
- no guru tone
- no fear marketing
- no shame-based language
- no unsupported claims
- no platform selection guidance
- no coding tutorial
- no startup jargon overload
- no business-coaching framing
- no market-research workbook framing
- no prompt-engineering tutorial
- no replacement for *Before the Build Starts*

This brief must preserve the Publication Concept’s:

- reason for existing
- core lesson
- mistake prevented
- reader transformation
- role in the product ladder
- “not this” boundaries

Editorial constraints:

- The booklet must help the reader decide whether the idea is ready to become a project.
- The booklet must not drift into detailed build planning, screen design, flows, data models, or implementation architecture.
- The booklet must not assume the reader has already chosen a platform.
- The booklet must not become a full startup-validation system.
- The booklet must preserve the distinction between:
  - deciding what the project should become
  - designing how the project will be built

Tone constraints:

- calm
- protective
- practical
- honest
- clear
- beginner-respectful
- confidence-building without being motivational fluff

---

## Reader Transformation Target

Before reading, the reader is likely to have enthusiasm, vague ambition, and an urge to begin building immediately.

After reading, the reader should have:

- a clearer problem statement
- a defined first user
- a smaller and safer version-one boundary
- a “not yet” list
- a clearer sense of whether the idea should move forward
- enough clarity to continue into *Before the Build Starts*

---

## Mistake This Publication Must Prevent

This publication must prevent the reader from treating AI-generated motion as proof that the idea is ready to become a real project.

The booklet should interrupt the pattern of:

idea → AI build request → visible output → emotional attachment → expanding project → avoidable cost and confusion

---

## Publishing Outcome Requirement

Publishing Factory should produce a booklet that feels like a practical field guide for deciding whether an AI app idea is ready to become a real project.

The final publication should leave the reader feeling more grounded, more selective, and more capable of saying:

- this is the problem
- this is who it is for
- this is version one
- this is what we are not building yet
- this is ready to become a project
- or this is not ready yet

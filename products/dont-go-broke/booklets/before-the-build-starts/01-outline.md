# outline.md

**Publication:** *Before the Build Starts*

---

## Field Note Opening

**Section Objective:** Set the tone and introduce the core premise that building without clear definition leads to costly mistakes.

**Lesson Being Taught:** *Slow down and think before you build.*

**Mistake Being Prevented:** Starting a build before you have defined the problem, user, and success criteria.

**Intended Reader Realization:** Recognize that thoughtful design is the safety net for any AI-assisted build.

**Estimated Page Count:** 2

---

## Section 1 – The Mistake Happens Before the Tool Choice

**Section Objective:** Explain why the most expensive errors arise before any tool or platform is selected.

**Lesson Being Taught:** Every mistake begins with a missing definition, not with the wrong tool.

**Mistake Being Prevented:** Choosing a tool or AI model before the problem is clearly articulated.

**Intended Reader Realization:** The reader sees that tool selection is a symptom of an undefined problem.

**Estimated Page Count:** 3

---

## Section 2 – Define the Job Before Defining the App

**Section Objective:** Guide the reader to articulate the real job the publication must accomplish.

**Lesson Being Taught:** Separate the *job* from a concrete app or AI prompt.

**Mistake Being Prevented:** Building an app that solves the wrong problem because the job was never defined.

**Intended Reader Realization:** A clearly written job statement anchors every later decision.

**Estimated Page Count:** 3

---

## Section 3 – Decide What Version One Really Is

**Section Objective:** Establish a minimal viable version that proves the core concept without excess.

**Lesson Being Taught:** Focus on the smallest functional slice that demonstrates the lesson.

**Mistake Being Prevented:** Over-engineering a first version before the core definition is solid.

**Intended Reader Realization:** The reader learns to limit scope until the definition is proven.

**Estimated Page Count:** 3

---

## Section 4 – Create the Blueprint Before the Build

**Section Objective:** Teach the creation of a visual/structural blueprint that maps the solution.

**Lesson Being Taught:** Blueprinting captures intent, flow, and constraints before any code or AI generation.

**Mistake Being Prevented:** Building without a shared, documented design, leading to rework.

**Intended Reader Realization:** The reader understands that a solid blueprint is the contract with the AI agent.

**Estimated Page Count:** 3

---

## Section 5 – Map the User Journey

**Section Objective:** Walk the reader through defining the end-user experience step-by-step.

**Lesson Being Taught:** User-centric mapping ensures the solution solves real pain points.

**Mistake Being Prevented:** Ignoring how the user will actually interact, which often stems from an undefined user profile.

**Intended Reader Realization:** The reader sees a clear journey that highlights gaps before any build starts.

**Estimated Page Count:** 2

---

## Section 6 – Define Agent Boundaries Before the Agent Starts

**Section Objective:** Set explicit limits on what the AI agent may generate, edit, or decide.

**Lesson Being Taught:** Guardrails keep the AI output aligned with the blueprint and safety goals.

**Mistake Being Prevented:** Allowing the AI to drift into undefined territory because boundaries were never set.

**Intended Reader Realization:** The reader feels empowered to give the AI a clear, constrained task.

**Estimated Page Count:** 2

---

## Section 7 – Prepare the Handoff

**Section Objective:** Consolidate the definition, blueprint, and boundaries into a safe handoff package.

**Lesson Being Taught:** The AI can only build safely when it receives a complete, well-structured handoff.

**Mistake Being Prevented:** Handing the AI an ambiguous prompt that lacks the necessary definitions.

**Intended Reader Realization:** The reader knows how to package the work so the AI can act without guessing.

**Estimated Page Count:** 3

## Section 8 – The Final Pre-Build Review

**Section Objective:** Conduct a narrative-driven review that validates the entire definition-to-handoff pipeline.

**Lesson Being Taught:** Verify readiness through story-based questioning, not exhaustive checklists.

**Mistake Being Prevented:** Proceeding to AI execution with hidden gaps stemming from an undefined problem.

**Intended Reader Realization:** Confidence that the project is ready for safe AI-assisted execution.

**Estimated Page Count:** 2

---

## Field Note Closing

**Section Objective:** Reinforce the "slow down and think" mindset and remind readers to revisit the blueprint before each iteration.

**Lesson Being Taught:** Ongoing reflection prevents drift back into rushed building.

**Mistake Being Prevented:** Repeating the same planning shortcuts on future projects.

**Intended Reader Realization:** The habit of pre-build contemplation becomes a reusable skill.

**Estimated Page Count:** 1

## Mandatory Validation Question

**Question:** *If all checklists, tables, worksheets, templates, examples, exercises, and frameworks were removed, would the publication still teach the lesson, the mistake being prevented, the relevant trade-offs, and the intended reader transformation?*

**Answer:** **Yes.** The outline's narrative structure places the core lesson, the warning about building before defining, and the description of the desired transformation at the start of every section. Even without any supporting artifacts, the reader receives a clear story that explains why premature building is dangerous, how to avoid it by defining the problem, user, and success criteria, and what the resulting transformation looks like: a disciplined planner who hands off a well-defined blueprint to an AI. The trade-offs — time spent thinking versus wasted effort building the wrong thing — are woven into each warning, ensuring the publication's educational intent stands on its own.
